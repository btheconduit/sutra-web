# Sutra — Context

## Purpose

Sutra is a minimal study tool for students of Vedanta.

It helps students quickly find Sanskrit terms, understand their contextual meaning, and stay anchored in the thread of study without interrupting contemplation.

This tool is not for learning Sanskrit in a general sense. It is designed to support ongoing Vedantic study.

---

## Primary Use Case

A student is listening to a lecture or studying a text.

They hear or see a Sanskrit term.

They open Sutra, search the term, and immediately:
- recognize the word
- understand its meaning in context
- return to study without losing attention

---

## Target User (V1)

- Students of Swami Tadatmananda's course
- Beginner to intermediate Vedanta students
- English-speaking users using transliteration
- Students studying alongside live or recorded teaching

---

## Core Principle

Do not interrupt contemplation.

Every design and feature decision must support this.

---

## Experience Qualities

The interface should feel:
- clear
- quiet
- airy

Avoid density, noise, or stimulation.

---

## Core Feature (V1)

Search Sanskrit terms and display:

1. Root meaning (if available)
2. Glossary definition
3. Transliteration
4. Source reference (if available)
5. Example usage (if available)
6. Contextual meaning (Vedanta-specific)
7. Related terms

---

## Source of Truth Hierarchy

1. Swami Dayananda glossary (primary)
2. Swami Tadatmananda usage (aligned with glossary)
3. Source text references
4. Curated contextual notes

No speculative or AI-generated meanings.

---

## Interpretation Policy

- High-trust, editorially controlled
- No community editing in V1
- Personal notes may be added privately by the user (later phase)

---

## Non-Goals

Sutra must not become:

- a gamified language app
- a generic Sanskrit dictionary
- a social platform
- an AI explainer tool
- a substitute for teacher or sampradāya

---

## Minimum Viable Experience

A user can:

- search a term like "adhyāsa"
- immediately see:
  - glossary meaning
  - contextual meaning
  - related terms

This must be fast, clear, and frictionless.

---

## Design Direction

- Minimal interface
- Fast search
- Immediate clarity
- No unnecessary UI elements
- No visual clutter

The tool should feel like a quiet extension of study, not a separate environment.
